## Intro (~60 seconds)

Olympic lifting is one of the most technical things you can do in a gym. The snatch and the clean and jerk demand mobility, timing, speed, and patience. If you have ever wanted to learn them properly, rather than just throwing weight on a bar and hoping, this programme is where I would start.

Olympic Foundations in Tempered Strength is a four-week, technique-first block. Three days a week, about sixty minutes per session. Monday is snatch focus, Wednesday is clean and jerk focus, Friday is full classic lifts.

It is free, marked intermediate, and the app is very clear about what it is not: week four builds to a heavy technical single, not a one-rep max test. Bar speed and movement quality set the limits, not ego.

Let me explain the four-week arc, what each day involves, and whether this is the right starting point for you.

## Main body (~5–6 minutes)

### The big picture

This programme treats technique as the product, not the side effect. Load is earned by movement quality.

**Week one: Technique week.** Empty or light bar. Building the pattern.

**Week two: Light build.** Roughly forty to fifty-five percent of your max, or whatever feels comfortable. Bar speed is the priority.

**Week three: Moderate build.** Sixty to seventy percent working weights. Same rep quality every rep.

**Week four: Heavy technical.** Seventy to eighty-five percent. Heavy but technically perfect singles or low reps. Not a max-out day.

Every exercise lists loading guidance in percentages and plain language, so you know exactly what the intent is even if you do not have recorded maxes yet.

### Day one: Snatch focus

Olympic lift warmup every session: cardio, mobility for wrists, ankles, and thoracic spine, then three sets of muscle cleans, front squats, and overhead squats with the empty bar. Do not skip this.

Overhead squat comes first as a positional primer for the catch. Then hang snatch as the main technical lift. Snatch high pull to overload the second pull and build the finish. Planks to close for bracing work that protects your spine under overhead load.

The order matters. Overhead squat before hang snatch, never after. You are grooving the catch position before you load the pull.

### Day two: Clean and jerk focus

The most demanding session of the week. Hang clean for the pull and elbow turnover. Split jerk for the overhead receiving position. Front squat to build front rack tolerance and leg drive. Seated cable rows for upper back strength that supports the front rack. Hanging leg raises for hip flexor strength used when pulling under the bar.

Front rack position is a common limiter for beginners. This day addresses it directly with dedicated squat volume and upper back work.

### Day three: Full classic lifts

Integration day. Full snatch and clean and jerk in their complete form, plus the heaviest pull of the week and posterior chain accessory work. In week four, this session peaks in intensity. You build to heavy but perfect singles. The moment technique breaks, you stop. Walking away from a missed lift is a win in a foundations programme.

### What to expect

Four weeks will not overhaul your physique. It will overhaul your lifts. Cleaner positions, better mobility through hips, ankles, and shoulders, and far more confidence under the bar in the snatch and clean and jerk.

You need bumper plates or a platform setup, a gym that allows Olympic lifting, and ideally some coaching mindset: film your lifts, move deliberately, and respect the process.

### Who is this for?

Good fit if you have barbell experience, decent mobility, and you want to learn Olympic lifts with structured progression. Less ideal if you are brand new to barbells, your gym does not allow dropping weights, or you want a physique or powerlifting programme.

If you complete this block and want to peak for new maxes, the Advanced Olympic Performance programme is the natural next step.
