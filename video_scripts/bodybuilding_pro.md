## Main body (~6–7 minutes)

### The big picture

Most gym programmes give you a few weeks of workouts and call it done. This one is built as a full mesocycle: four distinct phases across sixteen weeks, each with a different job.

Phase one is Foundation, weeks one to four. Higher reps, moderate load, form mastery. You are learning the movements and building work capacity.

Phase two is Hypertrophy, weeks five to eight. Volume peaks here. Loads climb in a controlled way. This is where most of the visible size work happens.

Phase three is Intensification, weeks nine to twelve. Rep ranges drop, loads go heavier, and AMRAP sets appear on selected isolation exercises. You are pushing closer to your limits while still controlling form.

Phase four is Peaking, weeks thirteen to sixteen. Lower volume, maximum load, strength-hypertrophy focus. You finish the block looking fuller and lifting heavier on every major movement.

Across all four phases, compound rep ranges taper down and accessory rep ranges taper with them. Rest times increase as loads go up. That is progressive overload built into the structure, not something you have to figure out on your own.

### Day by day

**Monday: Chest.** Dedicated chest day with a proper chest warmup: light cardio, pec and lat stretches, band pull-aparts, scapular push-ups, and light bench pressing to groove the bar path. You hit your main pressing movement, incline work, flyes or cable work, and tricep isolation. The session is designed to build chest size without neglecting the supporting muscles.

**Tuesday: Back.** Back warmup with thoracic mobility and light rowing to activate the lats. Horizontal and vertical pulling, mid-back thickness work, and rear delt attention. Back day is about width and depth: the classic physique look starts here.

**Wednesday: Legs.** The longest warmup in the programme, because leg day earns it. Squats or leg press as your main compound, Romanian deadlifts for the posterior chain, quad and hamstring isolation that alternates across the block, and calf work. Legs get their own day, which means real volume without rushing.

**Thursday: Shoulders.** Overhead pressing, lateral raises, rear delt work, and trap development. Shoulder health is built in: face pulls and rear delt exercises counterbalance all the pressing volume from chest and shoulder days.

**Friday: Arms.** A dedicated arm day. Bicep and tricep work with proper warmup for the elbows and wrists. By phase three, some arm exercises go AMRAP on the final set. This is where the sleeve-filling work happens.

### How progression feels

Intensity scales within each phase. You are not flatlining at the same effort for four weeks and then jumping. The programme ramps within each phase block, so week three of Hypertrophy feels harder than week one.

Machine and isolation exercises are swappable where it makes sense, so if your gym layout differs, you can adapt without breaking the programme logic.

### What to expect

The app describes it honestly: sixteen weeks is enough time to see real, visible change. Noticeable size across chest, back, shoulders, and arms. Stronger lifts on every major movement. By the Peaking phase you should look fuller and more defined, and your work capacity in the gym should be in a completely different place.

This is not a beginner programme and it is not a two-day-a-week maintenance plan. You need five sessions a week, a standard gym with barbells, dumbbells, cables, and machines, and the discipline to eat and recover properly.

### Who is this for?

This is for someone who has trained before, wants dedicated muscle group focus, and can commit to sixteen weeks. If you are brand new to the gym, start with the free Traditional Bodybuilding Split or the 3 Day Split first. If you can only train twice a week, look at the 2 Day Full Body programme instead.

This is a Pro programme in the app, so you will need a subscription to run the full block. But if you are serious about bodybuilding and you want structure that respects progressive overload, shoulder health, and long-term size gains, this is the flagship option.
