## Main body (~6–7 minutes)

### The big picture

Powerbuilding blends heavy compound strength work with targeted hypertrophy volume. You are not doing a pure powerlifting block and you are not doing a pure bodybuilding split. You get the best of both, organised across four training days.

**Phase one, weeks one to four: Accumulation.** Higher reps on compounds, eight to twelve range. Building work capacity and moderate loads.

**Phase two, weeks five to eight: Intensification.** Moderate reps, five to eight range. Heavier loads, strength focus increasing.

**Phase three, weeks nine to eleven: Realisation.** Lower reps, three to six range. Peak loads, AMRAP finishers on selected isolation work.

**Week twelve: Deload.** Reduced volume, lighter loads, testing where appropriate.

Exercise variants rotate every two weeks within each phase, so you get variety without losing the programme structure. Rest times increase as loads climb. Machine and isolation exercises are swappable.

### Monday: Upper strength

Heavy barbell compounds. Bench press, barbell rows, overhead press, and a vertical pull (chin-ups or lat pulldown). Face pulls or close-grip bench press alternate as the finisher depending on the week. This is your heavy pressing and pulling day. Low reps, long rest, maximum force production.

### Tuesday: Lower strength

Squat and hinge focus. Barbell back squat, Romanian deadlift, leg press, leg curls, and a core finisher alternating between planks and hanging leg raises. Lower strength day builds the numbers on your squat and the posterior chain strength that supports deadlifting.

### Thursday: Upper hypertrophy

Dumbbell and cable volume. Incline dumbbell press, rowing variations, shoulder pressing, lat work, cable flyes, lateral raises, and arm isolation. Higher reps, shorter rest, pump-focused. This is where the chest, back, and arm size work happens.

### Friday: Lower hypertrophy

Unilateral and isolation volume. Deadlift or front squat as the main hinge or squat pattern, Bulgarian split squats, hip thrusts, leg extensions, hamstring curls, calf raises, and ab work. Lower hypertrophy day builds leg size and addresses imbalances that heavy bilateral lifting can miss.

### What to expect

The app puts it directly: you will get bigger and stronger at the same time. Visible size across chest, back, and legs. Squat, bench, and deadlift numbers pushing to new levels. Twelve weeks is a serious block, and the three-phase structure means you are not just repeating the same weights week after week.

### Who is this for?

Good fit if you can train four days a week (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday), you have at least intermediate experience with barbells, and you want strength and size without running separate programmes. Less ideal if you can only train two or three days, want a pure powerlifting peak, or want a five-day bro split.

Surprisingly, this is free in the app despite being marked Pro in the name. That makes it one of the most comprehensive blocks you can run without a subscription.
