## Main body (~5–6 minutes)

### The big picture

Bootcamp here does not mean random exhaustion. It means circuit-based training with deliberate session design and progressive loading across six weeks.

**Weeks one and two: Foundation.** Learn the movements. Moderate loads. More guidance in the exercise notes. Four rounds on main circuits. Shorter finishers. Your first job is to own the hinge pattern, because everything else follows from that.

**Weeks three and four: Build.** Loads increase. Rounds may extend. Rest periods tighten slightly. You know the movements now, so the focus shifts to work capacity and load progression.

**Weeks five and six: Push.** Heaviest loads of the block. Longer circuits, tougher finishers. You finish six weeks having mastered the basics and progressed the weight.

### Session one: Hinge and pull focus

Full body with a swing and pull emphasis. The kettlebell carries this session.

Warmup one targets the posterior chain: halos, goblet squats, glute bridges, push-ups. Then a swing and pull circuit: kettlebell swings, rows, and complementary movements. The swing is the centrepiece. Power from the hips, not the arms. If the bell feels heavy in your hands, you are pulling with your arms instead of hinging.

Cooldown one focuses on hips, hamstrings, and upper back after hinge-dominant work.

### Session two: KB skill and upper body

Shoulder and upper back preparation in the warmup. Halos rehearse the skill work ahead. Overhead pressing, carries, rows, and kettlebell skill movements feature in the main circuit.

This session builds pressing strength and kettlebell competency. Cold shoulders do not press well, so the warmup earns its length.

Cooldown two targets shoulders, chest, and thoracic mobility. Thoracic rotation at the end is the most important stretch on this day.

### Session three: Lower body and Turkish get-up

The hardest lower body session of the week. Warmup three is five minutes: halos, glute bridges, squats, reverse lunges, inchworms. The Turkish get-up appears here, loading the shoulder overhead while demanding hip mobility, stability, and coordination.

Romanian deadlifts, split squats, step-ups, and calf work round out the lower body focus. Cooldown three is the longest: hip flexors, glutes, hamstrings, calves, and shoulders.

### What to expect

Full-body strength and improved fitness relatively fast. Better stamina, a stronger core, and more shape through legs, glutes, and shoulders as you master the basics and progress the load.

This is not a barbell strength programme. You will not max out your squat. You will get functionally strong, athletic, and confident with kettlebells and dumbbells.

### Who is this for?

Good fit if you train at home or in a gym with limited barbell access, you have at least one kettlebell and a pair of dumbbells, and you want three structured sessions a week. Less ideal if you want powerlifting or bodybuilding specificity, or you dislike circuit-style training.

You need a kettlebell (or two at different weights as you progress), dumbbells, and floor space for get-ups and ground work.
