## Main body (~5–6 minutes)

### The big picture

Six weeks split into two-week phases.

**Weeks one and two: Volume phase.** Compounds at eight to ten reps, intensity seven.

**Weeks three and four: Strength phase.** Compounds at five to eight reps, intensity eight.

**Weeks five and six: Peak phase.** Compounds at three to five reps, intensity nine. Rest times extend on the heaviest work.

Rep ranges taper every two weeks. Intensity climbs. This is a peak programme, meaning you should already have a training base before you start. The app is clear: expect strength to climb each block, not starting from zero.

### Day one: Chest and back (heavy)

Push-pull pairing on the same day. Barbell bench press and barbell rows as the main compounds, both using the phase rep range. Incline dumbbell press and seated cable rows for additional volume. This day is about upper body power: heavy pressing and heavy pulling in one session.

### Day two: Lower body (squat focus)

Barbell back squat as the primary mover, five sets, with intensity bumped one point above the weekly baseline. Leg press, leg curls, and planks. Squat day is the highest intensity lower body session of the week. In peak phase, the cue is a fast, aggressive ascent out of the bottom.

### Day three: Shoulders and arms

A slightly lower intensity day for structural balance and hypertrophy. Strict barbell overhead press, lateral raises, face pulls for rear delt health, hammer curls, and skull crushers. Face pulls are deliberate here: six weeks of heavy pressing without rear delt work creates imbalance and impingement risk. The programme addresses that.

### Day four: Lower body (deadlift focus)

Barbell deadlift as the main pull, with reps scaling through the phases like every other compound. Bulgarian split squats for hip stability. Hip thrusts for glute strength. Hanging leg raises for core work that does not add spinal flexion stress after heavy pulling.

Deadlift day intensity runs one point higher than the weekly baseline, matching squat day in demand.

### Day five: Upper body hypertrophy

The pump day. Dumbbell bench press, lat pulldown, dips, face pulls, and dumbbell bicep curls. Mind-muscle connection, isolation, and volume. Dips go AMRAP in week six. This day balances the week: four days of heavy compound focus, one day of hypertrophy finish work.

### What to expect

You will lift heavier and look the part. Real power added to your main lifts. Visible density through your back, legs, and shoulders. This is a six-week peak, not a year-long base-building programme. Run it when you want to push strength, then recover and either repeat or transition to a hypertrophy block.

### Who is this for?

Advanced lifters who can train Monday through Friday, are comfortable with barbell squat, bench, deadlift, and overhead press, and want a structured strength peak. Less ideal for beginners, two-day-a-week trainers, or anyone primarily chasing muscle isolation and physique splits.
