## Main body (~6 minutes)

### The big picture

This is not random circuit training. Every session follows a deliberate format.

A matched warmup prepares the specific muscles and joints for that day's work. Full body, upper, lower, power, or conditioning-specific warmups depending on the session.

A strength primer comes next. Controlled rounds of pressing, pulling, squatting, or hinging. Not for time. Move well, rest between rounds. This primes your nervous system and builds quality movement before the conditioning starts.

The WOD is the centrepiece. Named workouts with distinct formats: AMRAPs, ladders, timed stations, chipper-style sessions, accumulation workouts, and peak-effort finishers. By week five you are handling formats most people avoid. By week six you know exactly what you are capable of.

A proper cooldown closes every session. Walk first, then stretch. The app gives you specific stretches for the muscles that worked hardest.

### How six weeks progress

**Week one: Learn the formats.** Short, sharp WODs. Build the habit. Sessions include Ignition, The Ladder, Push Storm, and Ground and Pound, the same four as the taster but as the foundation for what follows.

**Weeks two and three: Volume and variety increase.** New named sessions appear: Death by Dumbbell, The Grind, Station Six, Burn Out, The Gauntlet. Formats get longer and more complex. You are building work capacity.

**Weeks four and five: Intensity climbs.** Sessions like Pressing Matters, Lower Inferno, Full Send, The Hurricane, Pull Everything, Leg Day Lies, Core Reckoning, and The Accumulator. Harder combinations, less rest, more mental toughness required.

**Week six: Peak week.** Final Ignition, The Last Pull, Six Weeks Done, and Peak. The culmination of six weeks of varied conditioning. You finish knowing your limits and how to push them.

### What to expect

Better stamina, more athleticism, and a tighter feel through legs, glutes, and core. This is a cutting and conditioning programme, not a muscle-building block. You will get fitter, leaner-feeling, and more capable in athletic movements. Pure strength numbers on barbell lifts are not the goal.

You need dumbbells for most sessions. Bodyweight works for many movements. Floor space for ground work. A willingness to train with elevated heart rate four times a week.

### Who is this for?

Good fit if you want structured conditioning, you enjoy varied workouts, and you can commit to Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Sunday for six weeks. Try the free taster first if you are unsure about the format.

Less ideal if you want a hypertrophy or powerlifting programme, you dislike high-intensity work, or you can only train two days a week.

This is a Pro programme, so a subscription is required for the full twenty-four sessions.
