## Main body (~5 minutes)

### The big picture

Every HIIT Shred session follows the same architecture: a targeted warmup, a strength primer, a named WOD at the centre, and a proper cooldown.

The warmup is not generic. Each session has a warmup matched to the stimulus. Full body, upper focus, or lower focus, with specific movements to prepare the muscles and joints you are about to load.

The strength primer is not for time. Usually two or three rounds of controlled strength work: dumbbell pressing, rowing, squats, or similar. This earns you the right to push hard in the WOD. You build a base of quality movement before the conditioning starts.

The WOD is where the session lives. AMRAPs, ladders, timed circuits, ground-based conditioning. Named workouts with clear instructions. Your heart rate spikes, your legs get heavy, and you learn to pace yourself rather than blowing up in round two.

The cooldown matters. Ninety seconds of easy walking before you stop moving entirely. Then targeted stretches for the muscles that worked hardest. Sitting down immediately after intense conditioning is one of the worst things you can do for recovery.

### The four sessions

**Ignition (Monday).** Your first session. Full body. The WOD is a ten-minute AMRAP: as many rounds as possible. The primer includes dumbbell bench press and dumbbell rows. Set a pace you can hold.

**The Ladder (Wednesday).** Upper body focus. The warmup prepares shoulders, chest, and upper back. A ladder format WOD where reps climb or descend in a structured pattern. Different stimulus from day one.

**Push Storm (Friday).** Pressing and pushing emphasis. More upper body volume in the primer and WOD. By this point in the week you know the format: warmup, primer, push hard, cooldown properly.

**Ground and Pound (Sunday).** Lower body and core focus. The lower body warmup is longer because these sessions punish cold muscles. Lunges, squats, and ground-based movements feature heavily. Finish the week with legs that remind you why you trained.

### What to expect

A taste of what the full programme delivers: better stamina, more athleticism, and a tighter feel through legs, glutes, and core. Four sessions is not a transformation. It is enough to know whether you enjoy this style of training and whether your body responds well to it.

You need dumbbells at minimum. Some sessions use bodyweight only. A bit of floor space helps for ground-based work.

### Who is this for?

Good fit if you want conditioning and functional fitness, you are bored of straight sets and rest timers, and you want to try before buying the full six-week Pro programme. Less ideal if you want pure strength or hypertrophy, you dislike high heart rate training, or you cannot train four days a week.

If you finish the taster and want more, HIIT Shred Full is twenty-four unique sessions across six weeks with no repeated days.
