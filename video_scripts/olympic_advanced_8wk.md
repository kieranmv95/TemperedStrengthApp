## Main body (~5–6 minutes)

### The big picture

**Weeks one to three: Accumulation.** Volume and positional strength at seventy to seventy-eight percent. Hang snatch in the early weeks to build the second pull and positional strength under load before switching to full snatch when intensification begins.

**Weeks four to six: Intensification.** Heavy doubles progressing to singles. Eighty to eighty-eight percent. Full snatch becomes the main lift on day one. Snatch balance drops out to keep session volume manageable as loads climb.

**Week seven: Realisation (peaking).** Ninety percent singles plus ninety-five percent openers. Volume drops sharply. You are sharpening, not accumulating.

**Week eight: Test week.** Day one is snatch one-rep max. Day two is clean and jerk one-rep max. Day three is a competition simulation: three attempts at each lift, a second shot at a heavier personal record. Accessories are stripped out. Warm up, lift, perform.

Every working set lists exact percentages of your current one-rep max, so you are not guessing loads.

### Session structure

Day one centres on the snatch and snatch variations. Day two centres on the clean and jerk and clean variations. Day three integrates both lifts with pulling work and posterior chain accessories through the accumulation and intensification phases.

The warmup is longer and more thorough than Foundations: five minutes cardio, ninety seconds each of wrist, ankle, and thoracic mobility, empty bar complexes, then ramping singles up to your first working weight.

In test week, the programme gets out of your way. Your job is to find new maxes, not to accumulate fatigue from accessories.

### What to expect

You will move faster under the bar and pull heavier off the floor. Eight weeks of structured peaking should sharpen your technique, build explosive power through hips and legs, and leave you ready to test new numbers in the final week.

This is not a body transformation programme. You will get stronger and more explosive. Visible physique changes are a side effect, not the goal.

### Who is this for?

You should have completed Olympic Foundations or equivalent coaching experience. You need current snatch and clean and jerk maxes to set percentages accurately. You need a gym with proper Olympic lifting setup: bumper plates, platform or lifting area, and space to drop weights safely.

Less ideal if you are still learning the movements, you do not have recorded maxes, or you want general strength or hypertrophy training.

This is a Pro programme, so a subscription is required.
