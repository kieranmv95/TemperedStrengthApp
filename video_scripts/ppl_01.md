This is a free, four-week hypertrophy block built around the classic push, pull, legs structure. Monday is push, Wednesday is pull, Friday is legs. Each session is about sixty minutes.

It is marked beginner-friendly, which means the layout is clear and the exercise selection is sensible. You will still work hard. But if you are new to structured training, or you want a proven split without committing to five or six days a week, this is one of the best entry points in the app.

## Main body (~5–6 minutes)

### The big picture

The programme rotates intensity and exercise variations to maximise muscle fibre recruitment. Over four weeks, intensity trends upward: weeks one and two sit at seven out of ten, weeks three and four move to eight. You are not just repeating the same workout; the barbell and dumbbell variation provides weekly contrast while the overall load climbs.

Goals are hypertrophy, bulking, and leaning out. Categories are bodybuilding. This is a physique programme first.

### Push day (Monday)

Every session starts with a standard warmup: light cardio, hip and hamstring stretches, arm circles, band pull-aparts, leg swings, glute bridges, and bodyweight squats.

Main pressing alternates weekly. Even weeks use barbell bench press with a controlled two-second lowering phase. Odd weeks switch to dumbbell bench press for greater range of motion and stability demands.

Shoulder pressing is a proper compound here, not a light accessory. Even weeks use strict barbell overhead press. Odd weeks use dumbbell shoulder press. Both sit in the eight to ten rep range with two minutes rest.

Cable flyes follow for constant tension through the chest. Then the finisher alternates: tricep pushdowns on even weeks, reverse flyes on odd weeks. Reverse flyes are shoulder health work after a full day of pressing. In week four, tricep pushdowns go AMRAP on the final set.

### Pull day (Wednesday)

Horizontal rowing alternates between barbell rows and dumbbell rows. Pull-ups are in every pull session with swap options if bodyweight reps are not accessible yet. Face pulls for rear delts and traps. Dumbbell bicep curls to finish, with AMRAP on the final set in weeks three and four only.

Pull day intensity now scales with the rest of the programme, so it progresses from seven to eight across the block rather than staying flat.

### Legs day (Friday)

The hardest session of the week, peaking at nine out of ten in week four.

Main leg compound alternates between barbell back squat and leg press. Dumbbell Romanian deadlifts for the posterior chain. Leg extensions on even weeks, leg curls on odd weeks, so you get direct quad and hamstring isolation without the programme becoming quad-dominant. Hanging leg raises close the session with AMRAP on the final set.

### What to expect

Four weeks of serious pumps and visible development across push, pull, and leg muscles. Tighter arms, fuller shoulders, stronger legs. The app is clear that four weeks is the start of real hypertrophy, not the finish line. Stack blocks like this back to back, eat enough protein, and the results compound.

### Who is this for?

Good fit if you can train Monday, Wednesday, Friday, you have access to a standard gym, and you want a push-pull-legs split without overcomplicating things. Less ideal if you want dedicated arm days, powerlifting specificity, or Olympic lifting.

Compared to the Traditional Bodybuilding Split in the app, this groups muscles by movement pattern rather than by classic body part pairings. Push-pull-legs is simpler to understand and slightly more efficient if you care about movement categories over bro-split tradition.
